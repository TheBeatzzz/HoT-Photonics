Photonics Science and Technology for Sustainable Living

Harnessing the power of light to build smarter, healthier, and more resilient urban environments for the future.

Discover Our Mission

Our Vision

To establish a national center of excellence that integrates interdisciplinary expertise to develop and deploy practical photonic technologies, addressing critical challenges in urban sustainability, from public health to environmental monitoring.

Hub of Talents

We unite leading experts from academia, government, and industry to foster collaborative innovation.

Sustainable Cities

Our focus is on creating tangible solutions for smart agriculture, urban safety, and environmental health.

Global Impact

Through international partnerships, we aim to position Thailand as a leader in sustainable urban innovation.

Core Research Areas

Our research is structured around five key pillars designed to enhance the quality of urban life.

Photonics for Social Well-being

Developing wearable biosensors, advanced optical imaging, and phototherapy solutions for elderly care.

AI-based Urban Surveillance

Enhancing public safety and security through intelligent, real-time monitoring systems.

Environmental Health Sensors

Using SPR, Raman, and hyperspectral imaging for precise monitoring of air and water quality.

Smart Agriculture

Integrating Photonics and IoT to improve precision, reduce costs, and promote sustainability in farming.

Industrial Innovations

Modernizing traditional industries and fostering deep-tech startups with novel photonic solutions.

Our Collaborative Ecosystem

Innovation is a team sport. We are proud to partner with a diverse network of leaders from industry, healthcare, academia, and government to translate research into real-world impact.

Industry & Commercial Partners

Bridging the gap between deep-tech research and market-ready solutions with our industry leaders.

Thai Optical Group Public Co., Ltd.

Collaborating on R&D of advanced optical metrology techniques to verify and enhance the quality of ophthalmic lenses during production, linking fundamental research with commercial manufacturing processes.

AML Technology Co., Ltd.

Partnering in the design and fabrication of high-precision optical instruments for laser systems and measurement, focusing on quality control applications in the agricultural and food industries.

CPF (Thailand) Public Co., Ltd.

Jointly developing Photonics for Smart Livestock Farming, including non-contact optical techniques for monitoring chicken health and egg quality, integrated with Deep Learning models for precision agriculture.

MLOptic Thailand

Applying photonics in advanced industries like AR/VR, Biometrics, and Autonomous Vehicles to accelerate technology transfer and commercialization.

K.P. Agro Korat

Developing high-precision Laser Projection Systems for creating large-scale 2D graphics on buildings and in agro-tourism sites.

Healthcare Partners

Working hand-in-hand with medical experts to develop and clinically validate next-generation diagnostic and monitoring tools.

Chiang Mai University, Faculty of Medicine

Developing and testing non-invasive photonic-based Capillary Refill Time (CRT) monitors for tracking blood circulation in critical care patients, aiming for deployment in community-level healthcare.

Suranaree University of Technology, School of Medicine

Collaborating across multiple specialties, including ophthalmology for screening eye diseases in the elderly, pediatrics for non-invasive skin assessment, and rehabilitation medicine for developing photonic vein finders.

Maharaj Nakhon Ratchasima Hospital

Partnering on the clinical evaluation and real-world testing of photonic prototypes for eye disease screening, ensuring technologies meet the practical needs of public health services.

Government Partners

Translating technology into policy and practice for national benefit.

Thailand Board of Investment (BOI)

Providing policy guidance and support for the commercialization of photonic technologies, connecting our research with industrial needs and promoting the growth of the photonics industry in Thailand.

Co-developing highly sensitive Surface-Enhanced Raman Scattering (SERS) chips for detecting contaminants in food and agricultural products by combining photonics with synchrotron light capabilities.

Community Partners & Public Engagement
